Four fashion post styles: daily OOTD (milk tea tones, person-featured), beauty tutorials (pink tones, product flat lay), outfit collections (white info poster), hauls/unboxing (product close-up, minimal background).

---

## Photography Tips

- **Clean background** — White wall, solid backdrop, minimal interior. Fitting room mirrors are an absolute no
- **Full body / half body** — OOTD must be full body; beauty can be half body or close-up
- **Natural light** — Side light by a window is best; avoid direct front lighting and overhead lights

In a good outfit photo, the clothes are the star — not your face, not the background.

---

## Color Palette

Cover tones should match the clothing:

- **Milk tea tones** — Cream, camel, khaki; perfect for daily office wear, soft aesthetic
- **Black, white, grey** — Minimalist cool, high-end feel; best for urban style
- **Pink tones** — Standard for beauty tutorials and sweet-style outfits
- **Earth tones** — The forever-safe choice for fall/winter outfits

---

## Title Formulas

- "One piece, X looks" — High utility, high save rate
- "Petite / midsize / pear-shaped friendly" — Body type targeting for precise reach
- "Everything under $XX" — Price threshold lowers decision friction
- "This X is so slimming" — Result-oriented, creates anticipation

---

## Post Structure

1. **Body stats** — Height and weight in one line, so readers can judge relevance
2. **Item list** — Brand + price for each piece, easy to replicate
3. **Styling notes** — 2-3 sentences on why this combination works
4. **Purchase links** — Include if available, not mandatory

!!! tip "Remember"
    The core of fashion posts is "replicability." Users should finish reading and think "I can wear this too" — so body info, prices, and brands are all essential.

# Part 1: Cover Design

Your cover image is the storefront of your Xiaohongshu post — users scrolling through their feed decide whether to tap in within a fraction of a second. A great cover doesn't need to be flashy; it just needs to be **clear, visually appealing, and compelling enough to earn a click**. In this part, we break down cover design into three pillars: composition, typography, and layout, giving you a systematic framework for creating covers that convert.

## Chapters

### 1.1 Composition

Master the most practical composition techniques to make your cover stand out in the feed.

**What you'll learn:**

- When to use rule of thirds, centered symmetry, and leading lines
- Why "less is more" — the power of negative space
- How to create visual hierarchy through contrast
- Common composition mistakes and how to avoid them

---

### 1.2 Typography

Learn to choose fonts, set sizes, and arrange text so that words enhance your cover instead of cluttering it.

**What you'll learn:**

- Serif vs. sans-serif and when to use each
- Recommended size ratios for headlines, subheadings, and body text
- How to pair text with images harmoniously
- The most frequent typography pitfalls and how to dodge them

---

### 1.3 Layout & Information Hierarchy

Understand visual flow and information density to create covers that are both beautiful and easy to read.

**What you'll learn:**

- How to establish hierarchy: headline > subheading > body > details
- Z-pattern vs. F-pattern reading flow and when each applies
- Layout strategies for different post types (tutorials, product reviews, travel guides)
- How to control information density on a single cover

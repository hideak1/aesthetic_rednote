# 3.3 Multi-Image Arrangement — Tell a Complete Story with Your Photos

Xiaohongshu posts can contain up to 18 images. But more images do not automatically mean better — what matters is the role each image plays and where it sits in the sequence. A well-arranged post is like a story: it has a beginning, a middle, and an end.

---

## Image Sequence Logic: Every Position Has a Purpose

Users pay different levels of attention to images depending on their position. Understanding each slot's role lets you maximize the value of every photo.

### Image 1 (Cover): The Make-or-Break Shot

The cover is the only image that appears in the feed. It determines whether users tap into your post at all.

!!! warning "How Critical Is the Cover?"
    The cover image accounts for over 50% of a post's success. You should spend more time on your cover than on all other images combined.

The cover has one job: **make people want to tap in.** It needs to —

- Feature a prominent subject with instantly clear information
- Have strong visual impact that stands out in the feed
- Work together with the title to convey the core message

### Images 2-3: Core Content Delivery

Once a user taps into the post, these are the first images they examine closely. They need to carry the post's primary value.

!!! example "How to Plan Images 2-3 by Category"
    - **Tutorials:** Show the key steps or core techniques
    - **Outfit posts:** Different angles — full-body shots and detail close-ups
    - **Reviews:** Real product shots and usage results
    - **Guides:** The most important information or actionable takeaways

### Images 4-6: Details and Supplementary Information

By this point, the user already understands your core message. Images 4-6 provide depth through details, comparisons, and supporting information.

Content that works well in these slots:

- Product detail close-ups
- Before-and-after comparisons
- The product or subject shown in different settings
- Informational graphics with supplementary details

### Images 7+: Wrap-Up and Engagement

Users who reach this far are already highly interested. This is the prime spot for driving engagement.

!!! tip "Smart Uses for Later Images"
    - A summary infographic (key takeaways from the post)
    - Product lists or purchasing information
    - Engagement prompts ("Which one do you prefer? Tell me in the comments!")
    - Related recommendations or series previews

## Visual Consistency Across Images

Arrangement is not just about content sequence — it is also about visual rhythm.

### Unified Color Tone

This was emphasized in the previous chapter, and it is equally important here. Swiping from image 1 to image 9, the color palette should feel continuous. A sudden style shift mid-post is jarring.

### Unified Style

Within a single post, shooting angles, composition approach, and editing style should all remain consistent.

!!! warning "A Cautionary Example"
    Image 1 is a carefully styled product shot, image 2 is a blurry casual screenshot, image 3 is a graphic downloaded from the internet — this kind of mismatch causes the perceived quality of the entire post to plummet.

### Visual Rhythm

Good arrangement has rhythm. Wide shots and close-ups alternate, information density varies between dense and light, colors echo and evolve. Like a piece of music, you cannot play the same note from start to finish.

!!! tip "A Simple Rhythm Formula"
    Wide shot (establish context) → Medium shot (show the subject) → Close-up (highlight details) → Wide shot (new angle or scene) → Repeat

## Portrait vs. Landscape vs. Square

The aspect ratio you choose directly affects how your images are displayed.

| Ratio | Dimensions | Advantage | Best For |
|-------|-----------|-----------|----------|
| Portrait 3:4 | 1080x1440 | Largest footprint in the feed, strongest visual impact | The default choice for most posts |
| Square 1:1 | 1080x1080 | Clean, grid-friendly layout | Information-heavy posts, product grids |
| Landscape 16:9 | 1920x1080 | Wide field of view, great for expansive scenes | Scenery, side-by-side comparisons, before/after |

!!! tip "The Best Default"
    **Portrait 3:4 is Xiaohongshu's golden ratio.** It occupies the most screen real estate in the feed and holds user attention the longest. Unless you have a specific reason to choose otherwise, go with portrait.

**One important reminder:** All images within a single post must use the same aspect ratio. Mixing portrait and landscape images causes the system to auto-crop, which can cut off critical parts of your photos.

!!! warning "The Cost of Mixed Ratios"
    Always preview your post before publishing. If you notice that key information has been cropped out, adjust the ratio and re-upload.

## Chapter Summary

- **The cover is the top priority** — it determines whether your post lives or dies
- **The first three images carry the core value**; later images supplement and drive engagement
- **Visual consistency** matters more than any single image being perfect
- **Portrait 3:4 is the optimal default** — keep all images in a post at the same ratio

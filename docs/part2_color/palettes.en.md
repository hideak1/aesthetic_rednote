# 2.2 Style Palettes

Theory covered — now for the most practical part: curated palettes organized by style, complete with hex codes, ready to drop into your covers, backgrounds, and text. Find the group that matches your content vibe and start using it today.

## Morandi Tones

The essence of Morandi colors: **every hue looks like it's been filtered through a layer of soft grey**. Low saturation, moderate brightness — gentle, sophisticated, and never loud. This is the quintessential "elevated" palette on Xiaohongshu.

!!! tip "Pairing Advice"
    Morandi tones are nearly impossible to clash with each other. Use a lighter shade for the background, a deeper tone for text, and pick any of the others as an accent.

**Best for:** Home decor, OOTD, lifestyle, cafe reviews

**Pairing notes:**

- Choose the lightest tone (like Milk Tea) for backgrounds; use dark grey or dark brown for text
- Avoid pairing with high-saturation colors — they'll break the muted harmony
- Add generous white space to keep the layout breathable

!!! warning "Contrast Pitfall"
    Contrast doesn't mean "split the canvas 50/50 with two loud colors." One color should dominate (60-70%), while the other serves as an accent (30-40%). Without this hierarchy, the result is chaotic, not striking.

**Best for:** Beauty tutorials, food reviews, streetwear, event promotions

**Pairing notes:**

- Let one color fill the majority of the canvas; use the other for titles or localized accents
- White or light grey can serve as a buffer zone to ease visual tension
- High-contrast palettes work better on image covers than long-form text layouts

!!! example "Typical Application"
    When using cream tones for a cover, set your text in **dark brown** (e.g., #5C4033) or **dark slate** (e.g., #4A5568) instead of pure black. Pure black is too harsh and breaks the softness.

**Best for:** Japanese-style OOTD, Korean cafe reviews, healing vlogs, journal sharing, dessert baking

**Pairing notes:**

- Keep the overall palette in the high-brightness, low-saturation range
- These tones mix and match freely without clashing
- Use dark brown or grey tones for text — avoid pure black
- Especially well-suited for vertical covers and maintaining a unified grid aesthetic

## Dark & Gold

Dark & gold communicates: **quality, power, professionalism**. A deep background with gold accents naturally conveys premium status. The go-to palette for tech, business, and masculine-coded content.

<div class="color-swatch" style="--swatch-color: #C9A96E">

  <span class="swatch-block"></span>

</div>

!!! info "Why Does Black & Gold Feel Luxurious?"
    Black represents mystery and power; gold symbolizes wealth and quality. Together, they create strong light-dark contrast, while gold's inherent warmth tempers black's coldness — resulting in a look that's both powerful and approachable.

**Best for:** Tech gadgets, business insights, men's fashion, luxury unboxing, finance content

**Pairing notes:**

- Use #1A1A1A as your background — it has more texture than pure #000000
- Reserve gold for titles, icons, dividers, and other small-area elements
- Charcoal serves as a transition tone to prevent the layout from feeling too binary
- Set body text in white or light grey to ensure readability

## Quick Reference

| Palette | Keywords | Saturation | Content Fit |
|---------|----------|-----------|-------------|

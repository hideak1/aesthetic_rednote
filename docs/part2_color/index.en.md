# Part 2: Color

Color is the heart of visual appeal on Xiaohongshu. A well-chosen palette instantly communicates your style and mood, making users pause mid-scroll to look at your cover. This part takes you from color theory fundamentals to trending palettes, matching rules, and hands-on tools for building your own color schemes.

## Chapter Overview

### 2.1 Color Theory

The color wheel, HSB model, color temperature, and color psychology — understanding these fundamentals is the first step toward great palettes. You don't need to memorize hex codes, but you do need to understand *why* certain colors work.

**Key concepts:** Color wheel, primary & complementary colors, HSB model, color temperature, color psychology

---

### 2.2 Style Palettes

From Morandi tones to creamy pastels, from high-saturation contrast to dark & gold — curated palettes organized by style, ready to use. Each palette comes with hex codes and recommended use cases.

**Key concepts:** Morandi palette, high-saturation contrast, cream tones, dark & gold

---

### 2.3 Matching Rules

The 60-30-10 rule, monochromatic gradients, complementary contrast — master these principles to create palettes that are both harmonious and layered. We also cover common pitfalls to help you avoid rookie mistakes.

**Key concepts:** 60-30-10 rule, monochromatic matching, complementary contrast, common pitfalls

---

### 2.4 Tools & Practice

A roundup of great color tools, from mobile apps to online platforms. Plus a step-by-step workflow for applying everything you've learned to every post you create.

**Key concepts:** Xingtu, Canva, Coolors, Adobe Color, Butter Camera

---

## Learning Path

We recommend reading the chapters in order, building from theory to practice:

```
2.1 Color Theory (foundations)
    ↓
2.2 Style Palettes (inspiration library)
    ↓
2.3 Matching Rules (methodology)
    ↓
2.4 Tools & Practice (execution)
```

!!! tip
    Short on time? Jump straight to **2.2 Style Palettes** to pick a palette that fits your style, then circle back to the theory later.

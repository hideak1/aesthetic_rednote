Four travel post styles: city exploration (cool architectural tones), natural landscapes (golden warm tones), hidden gems (muted Morandi), itinerary guides (clean white info poster).

---

## Composition Tips

- **Rule of thirds** — Place the horizon at the top or bottom third, never dead center
- **Leading lines** — Use roads, rivers, railings to guide the eye deeper into the frame
- **Foreground** — Add a foreground element (flowers, window frame, branches) for depth

A good travel photo makes viewers feel like they're "stepping into" the scene, not standing outside looking at it.

---

## Color Styles

- **City exploration** — Cool tones (blue-grey), emphasize architectural lines and urban feel
- **Natural landscapes** — Warm tones (golden), leverage golden hour lighting
- **Hidden gems** — Morandi palette (low saturation), convey quiet, healing vibes
- **Itinerary guides** — White background + bright highlights, info-first and easy to read

---

## Title Formulas

- "X in autumn/winter is unreal" — Create anticipation
- "99% of people haven't heard of this place" — Curiosity through exclusivity
- "X-day complete guide" — Practical, high save rate
- "Stop going to X, go to Y instead" — Contrast creates engagement

!!! tip "Remember"
    The core of travel posts is "immersion." Make viewers feel like they're already there — intentional composition, unified color grading, and atmospheric text are a hundred times better than "I was here" snapshots.

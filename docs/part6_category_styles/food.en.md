Four food post styles: cafe hopping (warm brown), dessert sharing (pink/cream), cooking tutorials (clean white), restaurant reviews (dark moody).

---

## Shooting Angles

- **45 degrees** — The most versatile food angle, shows depth and layers
- **Overhead/flat lay** — Great for multiple dishes or beautiful plating
- **Eye level** — Best for drinks, burgers, and tall foods to show volume

A good food photo should have food filling at least 60% of the frame.

---

## Color Palette

Food posts always lean warm. Add a 5-10% warm filter in post-processing to make food look more appetizing. Cool tones are the number one mistake in food photography.

---

## Title Formulas

- "Hidden gem X in Y neighborhood" — Discovery feel for cafe hopping
- "This X is insanely good" — Simple, direct excitement
- "All-you-can-eat for $XX" — Price creates value perception
- "Foolproof X recipe" — Standard for cooking tutorials

!!! tip "Remember"
    The core of food posts is "appetite appeal." Every visual decision serves this goal: warm tones make people hungry, close-ups trigger cravings, prices trigger action.

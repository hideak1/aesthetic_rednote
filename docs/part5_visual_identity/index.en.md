# Part 5: Visual Identity

Your posts look good — but can someone recognize them as *yours* before they even see your profile picture?

Visual identity is your "face" on Xiaohongshu. When a follower scrolls past a cover image and immediately thinks, "Oh, that's from XX" — that is visual recognition. It is not something you are born with; it is something you build, one post at a time.

In the previous four Parts, we covered cover design, color, imagery, and copywriting separately. This Part ties them all together and helps you find **your own visual language**.

---

## Chapter Overview

### 5.1 Building Visual Consistency

Consistency is the foundation of recognition. We will explore the three pillars — color tone, typography, and layout habits — and discuss how fixed presets, cover templates, and a cohesive profile grid make your content unmistakably yours.

**Keywords:** Tonal unity · Cover templates · Profile first impression

---

### 5.2 Finding Your Style

Start by studying creators you admire. Build a mood board, define your direction with three keywords, and give yourself room to experiment. Style is not a stroke of genius — it is a process with clear steps.

**Keywords:** Learning by imitation · Mood board · Style keywords

---

### 5.3 Style Evolution

Your style is not set in stone. As your taste matures and your content direction shifts, your visual language will naturally evolve. The key question is: how do you change without losing what makes you recognizable?

**Keywords:** Gradual change · Aesthetic growth · Core element retention

---

## Study Tips

!!! tip "This Part is different"
    The first four Parts lean toward technique; this one leans toward self-discovery. There are no right answers — only answers that fit you. Relax and enjoy the process of exploring your own style.

We recommend reading in order: 5.1 → 5.2 → 5.3. First understand the principles of consistency, then learn methods for finding your style, and finally discover how to let your style grow over time.

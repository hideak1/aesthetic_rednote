# 4.2 Body Text Rhythm

The title gets the tap; the body decides whether the reader stays. A well-written post reads like music — it has rhythm, breathing room, and flow. If your text is a dense wall of words, even the best content will make people scroll away.

## Paragraph Rhythm: Alternating Short and Long Sentences

Good body-text rhythm works like breathing — short sentences are the exhale, punchy and energetic; long sentences are the inhale, expansive and explanatory. Alternating between the two keeps reading effortless.

!!! example "Compare"
    **No rhythm:**
    "Today I want to share some cover design tips I have been researching for a long time actually cover design is not as hard as you might think you just need to know a few core principles and you will be able to make great covers."

    **With rhythm:**
    "Is cover design hard? Not really. Master a few core principles and even a total beginner can create polished covers. Today I am breaking down the techniques I have tested over months — simple, practical, and ready to use."

!!! tip "Pro Tip"
    - Keep sentences under roughly 20 words for the easiest reading experience
    - Use short sentences for key points; use longer ones for explanations
    - Break to a new line every 2-3 sentences to prevent visual fatigue

## Section Dividers: Visual Punctuation for Your Text

Xiaohongshu posts are closer to "visual notes" than traditional articles. The right divider style makes your information hierarchy instantly clear.

### Option A: Emoji Dividers

Use emoji as the visual anchor for each paragraph. Colorful and friendly — ideal for lifestyle and product-recommendation posts.

!!! example "Sample"
    ✨ Step 1: Pick your main color

    🎨 Step 2: Add 1-2 supporting colors

    💡 Step 3: Use a neutral tone for the background

!!! info "Best For"
    Product roundups, tutorials, everyday sharing. Keep your emoji style consistent — do not mix flowers, stars, and animals randomly.

### Option B: Blank-Line Dividers

The cleanest approach: simply separate information blocks with empty lines.

!!! example "Sample"
    Today I am sharing 3 cover layout tips.

    First: do not overload the text. One or two lines of key words on the cover is enough.

    Second: white space matters. Do not cram elements edge to edge.

    Third: alignment. Keep all text and images aligned in a consistent way.

### Option C: Numbered Lists

Perfect for how-to content and step-by-step guides — the logical structure helps readers locate information fast.

!!! example "Sample"
    1. Choose a template
    2. Swap in your own images and text
    3. Adjust colors to match your style
    4. Export and publish

!!! tip "Which One to Pick"
    - Lifestyle or product posts → emoji dividers
    - Opinion or emotional posts → blank-line dividers
    - Tutorials or how-to posts → numbered lists
    - Stick to one style per post for a unified look

## Information Density: One Point per Paragraph

This is the most common beginner mistake — cramming three or four ideas into a single paragraph. The result is exhausting to read and impossible to remember.

!!! warning "Avoid This"
    **Overloaded paragraph:**
    "For covers, pay attention to colors, do not use too much text, leave some white space, also font choice matters — I recommend Source Han Sans or Zhanku fonts — and for layout you should center-align everything."

    **One point per paragraph:**
    "Cover colors: 2-3 colors are plenty. Too many make it look chaotic."
    (new paragraph)
    "Keep text minimal: only 1-2 lines of key info on the cover. More than that and nothing stands out."

!!! info "The Sticky-Note Rule"
    Think of each paragraph as a sticky note — one note, one idea. If you find yourself chaining points together with "also," "and," or "by the way," it is time to split the paragraph.

!!! warning "Text Wall vs. Breathing Room"
    - **Text wall:** Long paragraphs, no blank lines, emoji overload, dense information → readers bounce immediately
    - **Breathing room:** Short paragraphs, blank lines between sections, clear information points, visual comfort → readers stay to the end

!!! tip "Quick Rules"
    - No paragraph longer than 3-4 lines
    - One blank line between paragraphs
    - Important points get their own standalone paragraph
    - Never use more than 3 emoji in a row

## The Opening: Your First Two Lines Are Make-or-Break

After tapping in, the reader's eyes land on the first two lines of your body text. If those lines fail to hook their attention, they leave.

!!! tip "High-Impact Openers"
    - **Create empathy:** "Do you agonize over your cover every single time? I used to, too..."
    - **Spark curiosity:** "After I started using this method, my saves tripled."
    - **Lead with value:** "Today I am sharing 5 layout tricks I have used for six months — every one of them tested and proven."

!!! warning "Openings to Avoid"
    - "Hi everyone, today I want to share..." — too flat, zero information
    - "A lot of people have been asking me..." — unless you are a major creator, readers do not care

---

## The Closing: Guide the Interaction

Your ending is not just "the last sentence" — it is your final chance to prompt the reader to take action.

!!! tip "Interaction Prompts"
    - **Ask a question:** "What app do you use for covers? Tell me in the comments!"
    - **Create a poll:** "Do you prefer Option A or Option B?"
    - **Encourage saves:** "If this was helpful, save it for next time you design a cover!"
    - **Tease the next post:** "Next up: color palette tips — follow me so you do not miss it!"

---

## Chapter Recap

Body text layout is not something you "just write and post" — it deserves the same care as designing a cover. Remember: rhythm, dividers, density, breathing room, opening, and closing — every element shapes whether readers finish your post or scroll away.

!!! tip "Practice Exercise"
    Find a viral post that feels effortless to read, then analyze it paragraph by paragraph: What divider style does it use? How many lines per paragraph? How does it open? How does it prompt interaction at the end?

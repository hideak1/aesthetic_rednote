## Five High-CTR Title Formulas

### 1. The Number Title

Numbers signal value instantly — "5 tricks" feels more concrete than "several tricks." Place the number at the beginning for maximum impact.

!!! example "Examples"
    - "5 Tricks to Make Your Cover 10x Better"
    - "3 Steps to Perfect Your Color Palette — Even as a Beginner"
    - "8 Must-Have Products Under $15 for Students"

### 2. The Pain-Point Title

Hit the reader's frustration head-on, making them feel "that is exactly my problem."

!!! example "Examples"
    - "Why Does Nobody Engage with Your Posts?"
    - "Your Layout Always Looks Messy? You Are Overlooking This One Thing"

### 3. The Curiosity Gap Title

Create a mystery the reader has to resolve by tapping in.

!!! example "Examples"
    - "I Finally Found the Best Layout Method for Beginners..."
    - "This Color Secret — 90% of Creators Have No Idea"

!!! warning "Watch Out"
    There is a fine line between curiosity and clickbait. Always make sure your content fulfills the title's promise.

### 4. The Comparison Title

Contrast creates tension — readers want to know "what is the difference?"

!!! example "Examples"
    - "Amateur Cover vs. Premium Cover — Where Is the Gap?"
    - "Beginner Layout vs. Pro Layout — One Detail Makes All the Difference"

### 5. The List / Label Title

A separator tag tells the reader exactly what the post is about and who it is for.

!!! example "Examples"
    - "Must-Read | Xiaohongshu Layout Guide for Beginners"
    - "Save This | 2024 Fall-Winter Outfit Inspiration Collection"

---

## Title Length: Aim for 15-20 Characters

Too short and there is not enough information; too long and the key message gets cut off. Front-load your key information.

!!! tip "Pro Tip"
    Preview your title on the actual app to make sure important words are not truncated.

---

## Titles with Visual Imagery

Titles that evoke vivid pictures are naturally more compelling. Add color ("caramel"), scenes ("date night"), and details ("got asked 3 times").

!!! example "Compare"
    - Low imagery: "Sharing an outfit idea"
    - High imagery: "This caramel-toned outfit got me stopped 3 times on the street for links"

---

## Title Pitfalls to Avoid

!!! warning "Common Mistakes"
    - **Clickbait:** Exaggerated title with hollow content leads to unfollows or reports
    - **Overly long titles:** Once truncated, the meaning is lost
    - **Zero information:** "Just sharing" gives the reader no reason to tap
    - **Title-content mismatch:** Tanks the reader experience

---

## Chapter Recap

Writing titles is a skill you can practice. Pause on every title that makes you want to tap and ask: which formula did it use?

!!! tip "Practice Exercise"
    Take your 3 most recent posts and rewrite each title using all 5 formulas. Compare which version pulls you in the most.
